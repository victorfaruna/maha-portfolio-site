Maha Jouini

AI Thought Leader | Pan-African Advocate | Founder of CHIFAA | AI Governance & Digital Transformation Expert

Maha Jouini is a Tunisian AI thought leader, researcher, and Pan-African advocate working at the intersection of artificial intelligence, ethics, public policy, and human development. Her work is dedicated to ensuring that AI serves people, strengthens institutions, and advances social justice across Africa and the Global South.

She is the Founder of CHIFAA, a survivor-led initiative developing responsible AI solutions for women affected by breast and cervical cancer across North Africa. Inspired by her own experience as a breast cancer survivor, CHIFAA combines ethical AI, multilingual accessibility, and community-centered design to ensure that technology supports dignity, inclusion, and equitable healthcare.

Maha serves as Vice President of AFRIA (Agence Francophone et Africaine de l'Intelligence Artificielle) and is a Research Fellow at the Global Center on AI Governance, contributing to the African Observatory on Responsible AI. She has also served as a researcher for the Global Index on Responsible AI (GIRAI), is a Stanford University Ethics, Technology and Public Policy (ETPP) Practitioner Fellow, and was selected as an Inaugural Microsoft Elevate Changemaker Fellow, joining a global cohort of leaders advancing responsible and inclusive AI for social impact.

Her professional journey spans Tunisia, China, Mauritania, and South Africa, where she has contributed to digital transformation, AI governance, strategic partnerships, and international development. Through her work with the African Union Development Agency (AUDA-NEPAD) and leading Pan-African institutions, she advocates for an Africa that is not only adopting artificial intelligence but actively shaping its future through ethical leadership, digital sovereignty, and homegrown innovation.

An internationally recognized voice on responsible AI, Maha was named among UNESCO's Top 20 Arab Women AI Changemakers and is the recipient of the 2026 She Shapes AI Award for AI Thought Leadership. Her research and publications promote human-centered AI, decolonized knowledge systems, digital sovereignty, and the integration of African and Arab philosophical traditions—including Ubuntu, Teranga, and Hikma—into global AI governance.

Her ideas have been featured by Elon University's Imagining the Digital Future Center, where she argued that "technology alone cannot guarantee justice" and that AI must be guided by wisdom, dignity, and human resilience.

Beyond policy, Maha is building a broader movement that connects technology with philosophy, culture, and empathy. Through HIKMA AI, she promotes an African vision of artificial intelligence inspired by the intellectual legacies of Timbuktu and Baghdad, demonstrating that Africa's greatest contribution to AI is not only data or talent, but wisdom.

Her vision is to position Africa not merely as a participant in the AI revolution, but as a global architect of ethical, inclusive, and human-centered artificial intelligence—ensuring that the future of AI is shaped by African values, knowledge, and leadership.
